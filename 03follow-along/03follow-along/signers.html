Who Were the Signers

Most Famous Signers

John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress, best known for his large and bold signature on the Declaration. His name became synonymous with "signature" in the United States.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States. Advocated for liberty, democracy, and individual rights.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned polymath, inventor, diplomat, and writer. He helped secure French support for the Revolutionary cause and was a key figure in shaping the new nation.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence, member of the Committee of Five that drafted the Declaration, and later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Leader of the American Revolution, cousin of John Adams, and an influential organizer of resistance against British policies, including the Boston Tea Party.
